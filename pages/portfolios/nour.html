Nour Color

Nour and I met near 홍대입구 and began to unravel through dialogue, what she wanted to get from taking photos. There is a bit of a narrative that occurs naturally when doing shoots which is one of my favorite things about collaborating. She told me how restrictive it was for her to grow up as not only a woman but also as a Muslim woman. Everything she shared with me was not with a malicious or resentful tone but with a sincerity and an honesty that is sharp, the only way truth can be sharp. It cuts through any inclination to assume anything and carries its own weight, presents itself without any help or embellishment. We shot a few pictures first with her hijab. These were some of my favorite. I got to see her the way everyone back home gets to see her. When we took the photos of her transformation however, holding the hijab out and then finally dropping it—it was impactful. It was special that I got to be the one behind the lens, photographing that instant in time.

Nour Black & White

Taking the pictures in monochrome felt like the epilogue to the dropping of the hijab. An echo of her former self and yet, the absence of color was like something frozen in time in the absence of space. The same way a memory seems not to take up any space. Nour was a radiant subject illuminating every location we found to take pictures. And moving from the golden-nebulous color film to the black and white scale, retold the same story from a different vantage point. A point at which movement could be seen but was still an echo approaching the present.
